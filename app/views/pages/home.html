Chalk Theory Bandits is a band that was formed by six undergrads of the University of Pennsylvania and is one of the only cover bands on Penn's 20,000 person campus. It was founded in October 2012 as simply a way for six amateur musicians to come together and play music. Against all odds, these time-strapped students put together a set from scratch and qualified for their first battle of the bands just five months later. At their most recent concert, their performance captivated an audience of over 300 people.

Chalk Theory Bandits covers a variety of genres, yet mostly focuses on trending college music for live performances. In past concerts they have covered popular tracks by pop artists including Bruno Mars, Katy Perry, Eddie Money, Hot Chelle Rae, and Neon Trees. However, the group is best known for its incredible arrangements of pieces normally not covered by bands, including songs belonging to electronic and dubstep genres. These include Alex Clare's 2012 summer hit, "Too Close," and Avicii's summer 2013 hit, "Wake Me Up."

There are scores of musical groups on campus, but there is only one Chalk Theory Bandits.

We'll see you at the stage.
